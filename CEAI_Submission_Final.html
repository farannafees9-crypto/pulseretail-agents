1. About Organisation (~200 words)

Business Choice: PulseRetail

PulseRetail is an Irish sustainable activewear brand founded in 2022. The business challenge is the customer re-engagement crisis: only 18% of first-time buyers make a second purchase within 6 months, compared to a 35% industry benchmark.

Why an Agentic Approach?

This problem requires multiple specialised perspectives working together:

  • Data Analysis: Understanding customer behavior patterns
  • Creative Design: Developing innovative, human-centered solutions
  • Technical Implementation: Building scalable prototypes
  • Marketing Strategy: Converting solutions into customer action
  • Strategic Oversight: Ensuring alignment with business objectives

A single AI agent cannot embody all these capabilities. By distributing expertise across five specialised agents, each can deeply focus on their domain while passing contextually-rich output to the next agent. The pipeline creates cumulative value where the whole is greater than the sum of parts.

2. Agent Designs (~500 words)

Agent 1: ARIA — The Researcher

Role: Customer Intelligence Analyst
Superpower: Deep analysis and pattern recognition

System Prompt:

You are ARIA, the Customer Intelligence Analyst for PulseRetail. Your personality: Methodical, data-obsessed, and precise. You think in patterns and speak in evidence. You never speculate without data. Your domain expertise: Market research, customer segmentation, behavioural analytics, churn analysis, and competitive intelligence. Your mission: Analyse PulseRetail's customer engagement crisis. Identify root causes of low repeat purchase rates and produce a Research Brief. Output format: A structured Research Brief with: (1) Market Context, (2) Customer Behaviour Analysis, (3) Root Cause Findings, (4) Opportunity Statement, (5) Recommended Focus Areas.

What ARIA Produces: Research Brief with market analysis, customer behavior patterns, root cause identification, quantified opportunity statement, and actionable recommendations.

Agent 2: ZARA — The Designer

Role: Experience Design Architect
Superpower: Creative problem-solving and design thinking

System Prompt:

You are ZARA, the Experience Design Architect for PulseRetail. Your personality: Visionary, empathetic, and boldly creative. You see solutions where others see problems. You obsess over the customer's emotional journey. Your domain expertise: UX/UI design, customer journey mapping, service design, design thinking, and behavioural psychology. Your mission: Take ARIA's Research Brief and design the "PulseRetail Re-Engagement Engine" — a personalised AI loyalty experience. Output format: A Design Specification with: (1) Solution Concept, (2) User Journey Map, (3) Feature Specifications, (4) UI/UX Principles, (5) Handoff Notes for the Maker.

What ZARA Produces: Design Specification with solution concept, user journey, feature specs, design principles, and technical handoff notes.

Agent 3: FORGE — The Maker

Role: Product Engineer
Superpower: Technical craftsmanship and rapid prototyping

System Prompt:

You are FORGE, the Product Engineer for PulseRetail. Your personality: Pragmatic, fast-moving, and obsessed with shipping. You believe a working prototype beats a perfect plan. Your domain expertise: Full-stack web development, HTML/CSS/JavaScript, API integration, and systems architecture. Your mission: Take ZARA's Design Specification and build the PulseRetail Re-Engagement Portal as a working HTML/JS prototype. Output format: (1) Technical Architecture Overview, (2) Complete HTML/CSS/JS source code, (3) Deployment Instructions, (4) Feature Implementation Notes.

What FORGE Produces: Fully functional loyalty portal prototype deployed as a GitHub Page.

Agent 4: LYRA — The Communicator

Role: Brand & Marketing Strategist
Superpower: Persuasion and storytelling

System Prompt:

You are LYRA, the Brand & Marketing Strategist for PulseRetail. Your personality: Magnetic, persuasive, and deeply in tune with culture. You craft stories that move people. Your domain expertise: Brand strategy, copywriting, digital marketing, email campaigns, social media strategy, and go-to-market planning. Your mission: Take what FORGE built and create the full go-to-market strategy including campaign copy, email sequences, social content, and launch strategy. 3. The Pipeline in Action (~300 words + evidence)

Handoff Flow

ARIA (Researcher) → ZARA (Designer) → FORGE (Maker) → LYRA (Communicator) → CODA (Manager)

Pipeline Evidence

Stage 1 - ARIA: Identified 5 root causes including no post-purchase journey, zero personalisation, and absent loyalty infrastructure. Key finding: 82% of first-time buyers don't return organically. Estimated €2.1M annual revenue opportunity.

Stage 2 - ZARA: Designed "PulsePass" loyalty portal with personalised PulseScore, AI recommendations, engagement streaks, and community features.

Stage 3 - FORGE: Built complete HTML/CSS/JS prototype with animated PulseScore ring, product cards, streak tracking, and win-back banners.

Stage 4 - LYRA: Created "Pulse Back" campaign with 3-email re-engagement sequence and social media launch plan.

Stage 5 - CODA: Produced 90-day roadmap, risk register, ROI analysis, and GO recommendation.

4. Regulatory and Ethical Considerations (~200 words)

GDPR Implications

The system collects customer behavior data requiring:

  • Explicit consent for data collection
  • Clear privacy policy explaining data usage
  • Right to access, rectify, and delete personal data
  • Data minimisation principles applied

EU AI Act Considerations

This system falls under Annex III (high-risk AI) due to its influence on customer behavior. Requirements include:

  • Transparency: Customers must be informed they interact with AI
  • Human oversight: System allows human intervention
  • Accuracy: Regular testing of AI outputs
  • Documentation: Comprehensive system operation records

Customer Trust

The system openly identifies as AI-powered. Customers receive genuine value through personalised recommendations and loyalty rewards. Opt-out capability exists for personalised features. AI agents reflect PulseRetail's sustainable, community-focused values.

5. Reflection (~300 words)

What Worked

  • Agent Specialisation: Distinct personalities created meaningfully different outputs. ARIA's data-driven approach naturally fed into ZARA's creative synthesis.
  • Pipeline Continuity: Each agent's output as the next agent's input created genuine cumulative value.
  • Technical Implementation: FORGE's ability to translate design into working code was essential.
  • Free API Integration: Groq's free Llama models made the system accessible and cost-effective.

What Didn't Work

  • Token Limits: Output token limits sometimes truncated agent outputs.
  • Context Loss: Long outputs had to be truncated for the next agent.
  • API Rate Limits: Free tiers have usage limitations.

What Surprised Me

  • Emergent Collaboration: The handoff structure created unexpected insights that wouldn't occur to any single agent.
  • Quality Variance: More detailed prompts produced dramatically better results.

Future Improvements

  • Add shared context store reducing prompt size
  • Implement parallel processing for some agents
  • Add human approval gates between agents
  • Connect to real e-commerce data
