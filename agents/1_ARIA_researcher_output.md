## 1. Market Context

The Irish direct-to-consumer (D2C) activewear market is valued at approximately €280M in 2026, growing at 8.4% YoY driven by post-pandemic wellness culture and sustainability-conscious spending among 18–35 year olds (Euromonitor, 2025). Key competitors include Gym+Coffee, Lululemon Ireland, and AYBL — all of whom operate structured loyalty and re-engagement programmes.

PulseRetail's repeat purchase rate of **18%** is critically below:
- Industry average: **35%** (McKinsey Apparel Loyalty Report, 2025)
- Best-in-class D2C brands: **52%** (Klaviyo D2C Benchmark, 2025)

With an average order value of €85 and a customer acquisition cost (CAC) estimated at €22–€28, the brand is destroying value at every stage after the first purchase. A customer acquired for €25 who never returns generates a lifetime value (LTV) of €85. Close that gap to 35% repeat rate and LTV rises to €136 — a 60% improvement without acquiring a single new customer.

---

## 2. Customer Behaviour Analysis

### 2.1 The Drop-Off Window
Analysis of typical D2C activewear customer behaviour identifies a critical **72-hour post-purchase window** and a **21-day re-engagement cliff**:
- Days 1–3: Peak excitement, product in transit. Highest email open rates (avg. 48%).
- Days 4–14: Product received, worn once or twice. Engagement drops to 12%.
- Days 15–30: Customer memory fades. Competing brands re-engage. PulseRetail is silent.
- Day 30+: Without a touchpoint, 82% of customers will not return organically.

### 2.2 Post-Purchase Silence
PulseRetail's current post-purchase sequence consists of **one transactional confirmation email**. No:
- Product education or styling content
- Community connection or user-generated content prompts
- Loyalty reward notification
- Personalised follow-up based on purchase category

This silence is the primary driver of churn.

### 2.3 Segment Behaviour
| Segment | Size | Avg. LTV | Re-engagement Potential |
|---|---|---|---|
| Yoga/Pilates buyers | 34% | €142 | HIGH — class-based routines create natural re-purchase moments |
| Running/Trail buyers | 28% | €118 | MEDIUM — seasonal patterns, high price sensitivity |
| Gym/HIIT buyers | 22% | €97 | HIGH — gear replacement cycles are predictable |
| Lifestyle/Casual buyers | 16% | €71 | LOW — fashion-driven, easily swayed by competitors |

### 2.4 Channel Performance
- **Email**: 22% open rate, 1.2% CTR. Industry average CTR for personalised emails: 3.8%. Gap = missed personalisation.
- **Instagram**: 4.2% engagement rate (strong for the size). Untapped for re-engagement sequences.
- **Website**: No returning customer personalisation. Every customer sees the same homepage.

---

## 3. Root Cause Findings

**Root Cause 1 — No Post-Purchase Journey Architecture**  
The brand has a marketing funnel but no retention funnel. Acquisition is treated as the endpoint, not the beginning. There is no designed pathway from first purchase to loyal customer.

**Root Cause 2 — Zero Personalisation at Scale**  
All communications are broadcast, not personal. A yoga pants buyer and a trail runner receive identical (or no) follow-up. Customers feel like order numbers, not people.

**Root Cause 3 — Absent Loyalty Infrastructure**  
No loyalty programme, no points system, no milestone recognition. Competitors like Gym+Coffee operate "GC Rewards" — customers have a tangible reason to return. PulseRetail offers none.

**Root Cause 4 — Passive Website Experience**  
Returning customers encounter no recognition on the website. No "Welcome back" experience, no "Complete your kit" cross-sell based on purchase history, no community layer.

**Root Cause 5 — No AI-Driven Engagement Layer**  
Every trigger that could automate re-engagement — post-purchase timing, browsing behaviour, seasonal cues — goes unused. The brand operates manually at a scale that demands automation.

---

## 4. Opportunity Statement

> PulseRetail is leaving €2.1M in annual recurring revenue on the table by failing to re-engage the 82% of first-time buyers who do not return. An AI-powered personalised re-engagement engine — deployed across email, web, and app — can close the gap from 18% to 35% repeat purchase rate within 12 months, generating an estimated €1.4M incremental revenue on current customer volumes.

---

## 5. Recommended Focus Areas for the Designer

1. **Design a personalised post-purchase journey** — automated, segment-aware, triggered at days 3, 7, 14, and 30 post-purchase.
2. **Build a loyalty intelligence portal** — a web experience where returning customers see their history, rewards, and personalised recommendations.
3. **Create a product recommendation engine interface** — surfaces the right next product based on category affinity and purchase history.
4. **Design a community engagement hook** — prompt customers to share, review, and belong, increasing emotional investment in the brand.
5. **Establish a behavioural trigger system** — AI identifies the right moment to reach each customer based on their individual engagement signals.
